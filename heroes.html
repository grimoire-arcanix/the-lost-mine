The Company of the Story

Six figures stood at the heart of the tale: nobles, wanderers, rogues, priests, archers, and mages drawn together by chance, fate, and the call of the Lost Mine. Hover over each portrait to reveal the story they carried into the dark.

Lady Iris Vicaria

Human Fighter

Lady Iris Vicaria was born to a family that once held wealth, title, and standing in the hills of Corlinn Hill, before the eruption of Mount Hotenow reduced that inheritance to memory. Raised in comfort rather than splendor, Iris grew into a woman convinced that nobility meant more than an empty name. She saw in Phandalin not a shabby frontier town, but a place that could be disciplined, restored, and civilized. During the campaign, she endured ambush, battle, and near death, helped cast down Glass Staff, ventured into Cragmaw Castle and Wave Echo Cave, and proved herself worthy of the judgment of Lady Aldith Tressendar, who entrusted her with a sword and a legacy. In the epilogue, Iris restored Tressendar Manor and assumed the role of noble guardian of Phandalin, establishing the Vicarians as the house most closely tied to the town’s future.

Rev. Flint Rockseeker

Dwarf Cleric

Rev. Flint Rockseeker was trained as a soldier on Mintarn and later served in Neverwinter, where he grew disillusioned with men who mistook authority for virtue. When conscience placed him at odds with command, he turned instead toward faith. A cousin to Gundren, Tharden, and Nundro Rockseeker, Flint’s path led him not only toward justice in Phandalin, but toward his own bloodline’s buried legacy. He was central in breaking the Redbrands, winning the blessing of Daran Edermath, surviving the descent into Wave Echo Cave, and claiming Lightbringer from the depths of the lost temple. After the campaign, Flint used that holy weapon to reconsecrate the shrine of Dumathoin, helped cleanse the mine of the undead, and became the head priest of a new temple established in the restored mine, guiding Rockseeker stewardship toward prosperity rather than greed.

Seraphina Thumbleton

Halfling Rogue

Seraphina Thumbleton came to Phandalin in search of opportunity and found it among the Redbrands, where sharp fingers and sharp instincts paid better than honesty ever had. Yet fortune turned. Betrayed from within the gang and nearly killed on the order of Glasstaff, she fled with little more than her nerve and Tymora’s favor. She returned to the region carrying equal parts charm, resentment, and unfinished business. During the journey she outwitted goblins, earned the dagger of Klarg, exposed Lady Hilda, and helped bring down the Black Spider’s schemes. Her past never stopped shadowing her, but it also taught her how to survive where others would be swallowed whole. In the aftermath, Halia Thornton recognized Seraphina’s talent and drew her into the Zhentarim, where she rose through the Black Network and took control of the smuggling routes once left behind by Hale Hilda.

Magus Quarion Andvang

High Elf Wizard

Magus Quarion Andvang devoted his life to Oghma, all-seeing lord of knowledge, and spent long years in study, convinced that learning existed for the benefit of all and not for tyrants to hoard. Visions led him toward a desecrated altar in what had become Cragmaw Castle, and from there into the wider current of the Lost Mine’s history. Quarion’s purpose was never merely academic; he pursued knowledge with the belief that sacred places must be restored and truth returned to its rightful light. Though separated from the party for part of the campaign and later rescued from the hold of Cragmaw, his cause endured. In the epilogue, Quarion helped reclaim the old stronghold, reconsecrated the lost shrine of Oghma, and saw Castle Crags restored as a sanctuary for the faithful and for those imperiled in the Neverwinter Woods.

Robin

Human Archer

Robin was born to the memory of a better life. His family had once belonged to Thundertree, and after disaster scattered them, he grew up among laborers and dockworkers, carrying in him the stubborn certainty that he was meant for more. He longed to become the sort of hero who could stand against monsters, tyrants, and ruin itself. That dream turned to grief when Venomfang descended upon Thundertree and slaughtered its elves, leaving Robin bereaved and displaced once more. Even so, he endured. He joined the company that followed Gundren’s trail, faced goblins, Redbrands, Cragmaw, and the terrors beneath Wave Echo Cave, and emerged not merely alive but tempered. After the campaign, Robin began gathering allies and resolve in equal measure, preparing with his wolf companion to reclaim Thundertree from the dragon that made a ruin of it.

Hammer

Half-Orc Barbarian

Hammer arrived in the story already half legend: a lone half-orc warrior with a reputation, a broken love behind him in Waterdeep, and a restless hunger for danger, meaning, and perhaps affection enough to steady him. Boredom and heartbreak eventually drove him into the service of King Grol, though loyalty never sat comfortably upon him. When the moment came, Hammer chose the uncertain road of adventure over the stale obedience of Cragmaw. He cast his lot in with the party as they moved toward Wave Echo Cave, and his presence helped tilt the balance in their favor. In the years that follow, Hammer becomes a figure strangely revered by goblins and orcs alike, inheriting the rough obedience of a people left leaderless after the fall of Klarg and Grol. Yet few believe he will remain long in that role; sooner or later, restlessness will take him onward again.
