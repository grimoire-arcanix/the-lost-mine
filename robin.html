Robin

The would-be hero who became one through grief and resolve

Robin was born to the memory of a better life. His family had once belonged to Thundertree, and when disaster scattered them, he grew up among laborers and dockworkers, carrying with him the stubborn sense that he was meant for more. He longed to become the kind of hero who stands against monsters, tyrants, and ruin itself, even if the world gave him no reason to believe such things were possible.

He spent years in Neverwinter doing hard work at the docks, but the old pull of Thundertree never left him. That longing hardened into personal grief when Venomfang descended upon the ruined settlement and slaughtered its elves, leaving Robin bereaved and uprooted once more. Even so, he did not break. He joined the company that followed Gundren’s trail, survived the goblin ambush, the turmoil in Phandalin, the dangers of Cragmaw, and the darkness beneath Wave Echo Cave. Through hardship, he became the sort of hero he had once only imagined.

After the campaign, Robin began gathering allies and strength to reclaim Thundertree. With his wolf companion at his side and vengeance sharpened into purpose, he set himself against the shadow of Venomfang. What was once a young man’s dream of greatness became something heavier and truer: a vow to restore a lost homeland and to answer ruin not with fear, but with steel, patience, and the stubborn courage of one who refuses to let the past remain broken forever.
