A Tale on the Sword Coast

The following details the entire story of The Lost Mine of Phandelver as scribed by the High Wizard of Neverwinter. It consists of three parts, the prologue which the reader must understand as that sets the stage for all the events to come next, the events of the Lost Mine in the year 1491 D.R. (For those unfamiliar, D.R. stands for Dale Reckoning, the year when the great standing stone was raised by my people, the Cormanthyr elves, in the northern Dalelands.), as well as an epilogue and an account of the many plots and intrigues to come. Years are mentioned in most of the prologue, and all the names of various characters are taken from historical annexes. The prologue mainly plays out as a set of years and corresponding events, and the epilogue as a brief glimpse of things to come, the main expedition details the adventures of Rev. Flint Rockseeker, Lady Iris Vicaria, Magus Quarion And-vang, Miss Seraphina Thumbleton and two warriors simply called Hammer and Robin that led to the discovery of the Mine.

Part I: Prologue

637 DR

Year 637 D.R.

The Vision of Prince Maenoth

The story begins in the Kingdom of Phanorm, the Empire of Three Crowns. Prince Maenoth Kervellen, an elf of the royal bloodline and a powerful wizard in his own right, receives a vision from Oghma of a great power residing in the Sword Mountains. Following this revelation, the prince departs for the Neverwinter Woods with his protector Ser Alaric Tressendar and a large retainer of farmers, merchants, and workers.

638 DR

Year 638 D.R.

Castle Cragmaw and Phandelver Founded

Prince Maenoth lays the foundation for Castle Cragmaw, which in earlier years had simply been known as Castle Crags, along with an altar and shrine to Oghma within it. The workers raise the seven spires of the castle in the architecture of Phanorm. Nearby, Ser Alaric establishes a farming settlement and names it Phandelver, after his old home and his new purpose.

640–650 DR

Years 640–650 D.R.

A Bustling Settlement Rises

While the castle is being built, the settlement attracts many settlers and grows into a bustling town. Ser Alaric builds his manor atop a hill and takes up the faith of Tymora. Dwarves and gnomes devoted to Tymora and Oghma join the prince’s cause and aid the search for the lost power nearby. By the end of the decade, the castle stands complete.

650–700 DR

Years 650–700 D.R.

The Search in the Sword Mountains

The dwarves, led by the prince, search tirelessly for the lost power of Oghma. They brave the bitter snow of the Sword Mountains and fend off roving orc bands. Yet darker and more terrible powers are already watching these efforts from afar.

700–725 DR

Years 700–725 D.R.

Ice Spire Hold and the Hidden Cavern

Dwarven leadership under Lady Delsandra Amzarr establishes the fortress of Ice Spire Hold, which becomes the chief dwarven stronghold in the Sword Mountains. During this period, the dwarves achieve a major breakthrough: they discover a cavern with natural waves and hot springs, a place where the weave itself seems innately stronger.

730–790 DR

Years 730–790 D.R.

Trade, Towns, and the Forge’s Foundations

Prince Maenoth dies of illness, yet the region prospers as trade along the Triboar Trail expands through Phandelver. Merchant guilds and nearby settlements arise. Thundertree is founded by druids. The noble Vicaria family, owners of precious mines, establishes Corlinn Hill at the foot of Mount Hotenow. The Rockseeker clan takes up prospecting. Meanwhile, dwarves excavate the newly found cavern and gnomish followers of Oghma begin constructing a mechanism that will channel its power into a Forge of Spells.

811 DR

Year 811 D.R.

The Phandelver Pact

The Spell Forge is completed. After long negotiation, dwarves and gnomes agree to work together and form the Phandelver Pact, an alliance with the nearby settlement to share the riches of the mine for the peace and prosperity of all, and to honor the legacy of the late prince and of Ser Alaric, whose descendants now rule Tressendar Manor and Phandalin.

c. 815 DR

Year Circa 815 D.R.

The Birth of the Black Spider

Beneath the Kryptgaarden Forest, in lost mines, a dark elf named Nezznar is born. In the years that follow he masters arcane magic and, under the influence of his matron deity Lolth, takes the name “The Black Spider.” In time he too will seek a great magical bounty nearby.

c. 890 DR

Year Circa 890 D.R.

Dawnbringer and the Thayan Interest

The mine begins to yield great magical wealth. The dwarves establish a temple to Dumathoin, god of mining, within the cave. Human priests of the Morninglord join the forge and offer their smiths in exchange for the creation of a weapon capable of smiting the undead. Thus begins the forging of the Dawnbringer. Rumors of this weapon reach Thay, and Szass Tam assigns the mage Mormesk to investigate.

908 DR

Year 908 D.R.

Wealth Draws Predators

Ice Spire Hold, Thundertree, and Phandelver begin to accumulate enough wealth to attract dangerous attention. It is no longer only orcs that watch the region. Dragons, newly active in the locality, begin to set their sights upon it.

926 DR

Year 926 D.R.

Talon Given to the Black Hawk

Lady Aldith Tressendar, an elven descendant of Lord Alaric, assumes leadership of Phandelver. As a gift, the gnomes of the mine present her with the greatsword Talon, a fitting weapon for the knight known as the “Black Hawk.”

938 DR

Year 938 D.R.

The Cult Closes In

The Cult of the Dragon grows interested in the region as dragon activity increases. The faction that reveres undead dragons aligns with the necromancers of Thay under Mormesk, who silently makes his way toward the cave in search of an opportunity to strike.

939 DR

Year 939 D.R.

The Year of Winter’s Wrath

Orcs from the Kryptgaarden Forest, led by the warlord chieftain Kra, launch an all-out assault on Ice Spire Hold, overwhelming its dwarven defenders. At the height of the battle, the white dragon Cryovain descends upon the defenseless fortress. As dwarves perish and orcs are seized by winter madness, the horde sweeps onward into the Sword Mountains and next assails the Phandelver Mine. There Mormesk, with his Red Wizards and dragon cultists, seizes his chance and strikes. The resulting bloodbath collapses the cave and the mine is lost. Trapped within are Mormesk, dwarven and gnomish defenders, and orcish warriors locked in slaughter. The surviving orcs then fall upon Phandelver. Lady Aldith, unable to save the town, makes her last stand at her manor and dies defending the tomb of her parents in the mines beneath it. With her, Talon is lost. The invasion sweeps onward to Castle Crags, which is likewise overrun and later becomes Cragmaw. The town is massacred, the mine and its location are lost to time, and Cryovain earns the title “Winter’s Wrath.” The peaks thereafter are plunged into a cruel and enduring winter.

950–1450 DR

Years 950–1450 D.R.

Five Centuries of Loss and Hidden Schemes

The mine remains lost for over five hundred years while Winter’s Wrath slumbers. Orcs occupying Castle Crags rename it Cragmaw and establish the Cragmaw Tribe. The Triboar Trail lies dormant for centuries. Deep within the collapsed mine, Mormesk attempts to use the Spell Forge to gain immortality, but his knowledge proves incomplete. Instead of becoming a lich, he is transformed into a crazed and powerful Flameskull. He raises corpses as zombies and wraiths, creating his own undead host and continuing his cursed work. The weapon he sought, the Lightbringer, remains safely hidden in the Temple of Dumathoin, forever beyond his reach. During this long period, Nezznar learns of Mormesk from old tomes, joins the Red Wizards, and becomes increasingly interested in the mines. In time he bends the Cragmaw Tribe to his will. Near the end of this age, Elmar Barthen and Toblen Stonehill arrive as prospectors and set up camp at the ruined site of old Phandelver. Joined later by the corpulent noble Harbin Wester, they establish the town of Phandalin. Toblen and Elmar concern themselves with the Stonehill Inn and Barthen’s Provisions, while Harbin styles himself townmaster.

1462 DR

Year 1462 D.R.

The Prophecy Stirs the Dragons

The elements begin to rage as Winter’s Wrath awakens at Ice-Spire Peak. The Cult of the Dragon becomes deeply involved in the region, believing that the unrest is caused by slumbering dragons answering an ancient prophecy: “None shall be left save shattered thrones and the dead, Dragons shall rule the world entire” — or, by another reading, “None shall be left save shattered thrones, and the dead Dragons shall rule the world entire.” Both factions of the cult move into the area. Dragon activity is reported in the Neverwinter Woods, the Ice Spire Mountains, and at the fiery heart of Mount Hotenow. Promising to slay and raise these dragons, Nezznar forms an alliance with the dragon cultists, who agree in turn to help him find and control Wave Echo Cave.

1463 DR

Year 1463 D.R.

Mount Hotenow Erupts

The red dragon beneath Mount Hotenow awakens, causing the volcano to erupt. The disaster destroys the estate of Corlinn Hill, forcing the Vicaria family to flee to Neverwinter. With dragon cult activity increasing, more factions are drawn into the region.

1465–1480 DR

Years 1465–1480 D.R.

Phandalin’s Factions Take Shape

The Order of the Gauntlet is mobilized, and Daran Edermath, a paladin of Bahamut, comes to Phandalin under the guise of a retired knight and farmer. Linene Graywind establishes the Lionshield Coster. As Phandalin grows, its residents uncover the shrine to Tymora built by Ser Alaric Tressendar. The shrine appears in a vision to Sister Garaele, a Harper and devotee of Tymora, who travels to Phandalin, forms her own network, and reconsecrates the shrine. A gang of humans and halflings emerges in the town as the Redbrands. In response, Halia Thornton of the Zhentarim establishes the Miner’s Exchange to contest influence. Far to the north, the druid Reidoth leaves Thundertree to wander the region and befriends the farmer Qelline Alderleaf while staying for a time in Phandalin.

1482 DR

Year 1482 D.R.

To Be Continued

This year stands ready for the next part of the prologue.
