Lady Iris Vicaria

The noble who turned a ruined frontier into a seat of order

Iris was born to a family that once held wealth, title, and standing in the hills of Corlinn Hill, before the eruption of Mount Hotenow reduced that inheritance to memory. Raised in comfort rather than splendor, she grew into a woman convinced that nobility meant more than an empty name. Where others saw only a ruined inheritance and a diminished title, Iris saw duty.

That sense of duty drew her toward Phandalin, a place she believed could be disciplined, restored, and civilized. She did not come west merely to observe events, but to shape them. During the campaign, she endured the ambush on the road, the turmoil of the Redbrands, the intrigues of Cragmaw Castle, and the darkness of Wave Echo Cave. She stood firm when others faltered, survived a near-fatal struggle against Lady Hilda, and proved herself worthy in the sight of Lady Aldith Tressendar, who entrusted her with both sword and legacy.

In the years that followed, Iris restored Tressendar Manor and assumed her place as the noble guardian of Phandalin. Under her hand, the Vicaria name ceased to be a relic of a lost estate and became something living again, tied not to vanished lands, but to a town with a future worth defending. Through order, resolve, and the burden of inherited honor, Iris became precisely what she had long believed she was meant to be: not the heir to a dead place, but the ruler of one reborn.
