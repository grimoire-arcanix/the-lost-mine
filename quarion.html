Magus Quarion Andvang

The scholar of Oghma who turned ruined stone back into sanctuary

Quarion devoted his life to Oghma, god of knowledge, and spent long years studying the lore of the multiverse. He believed that learning should be shared, cultivated, and defended, not buried beneath desecrated altars or hoarded by the cruel. His faith was not passive scholarship, but living service to memory, meaning, and the sacred work of restoration.

Through visions received in trance and devotion, Quarion learned of a defiled shrine in the goblin-held ruin now called Cragmaw Castle. Once sacred to Oghma, it had been profaned and forgotten. That offense drew him toward Phandalin, where the fate of the shrine became entangled with the wider struggle around the Lost Mine of Phandelver. Though his path crossed the company unevenly, Quarion’s purpose never dimmed. He pursued truth in dangerous places and bore himself as a servant of knowledge even where ignorance ruled by violence.

After the party’s victory, Quarion was rescued from Cragmaw Castle by the combined strength of the Lords’ Alliance and the Order of the Gauntlet. With King Grol defeated, the long-lost shrine to Oghma was uncovered within the old stronghold. Quarion reconsecrated it, and Castle Crags — no longer merely Cragmaw — became once more a place of refuge, passage, and learning for those endangered by the Neverwinter Woods. In him, study had finally become sanctuary.
