Rev. Flint Rockseeker

The conscience-driven priest who reclaimed the mine of his bloodline

Flint was trained as a soldier on Mintarn and later served in Neverwinter, where he grew disillusioned with men who mistook authority for virtue. When conscience placed him at odds with command, he turned instead toward faith. Though still bound by rank and memory to the life he had left behind, he had already begun to understand that true strength was not measured by obedience, but by what one was willing to defend.

News of the Redbrands in Phandalin drew him westward, for in them he recognized the same cruelty he had come to despise elsewhere. He also carried a deeper tie to the land than he first understood, for he was kin to Gundren, Tharden, and Nundro Rockseeker. During the campaign, Flint helped break the Redbrand menace, won the blessing of Daran Edermath, survived the dangers of Cragmaw Castle, and descended into the buried sanctity of Wave Echo Cave. There he stood at the heart of the final struggle and claimed Lightbringer from the ancient temple below.

After the campaign, Flint used Lightbringer to reconsecrate the shrine of Dumathoin in the lost mine. Dwarven and gnomish priests entered under his protection, cleansed the tunnels of undead, and brought Mormesk to his end. A temple rose within the restored mine, and Flint became its head priest, guiding the Rockseekers toward stewardship, honest trade, and shared prosperity with Phandalin. In the end, he did not merely reclaim a mine. He reclaimed a legacy and gave it a soul.
